Robust smoothing: median vs Kalman

One dial, from a bounded-influence median filter to the ordinary Kalman smoother.

The smoother's measurement spring has energy $\tfrac12\phi(x_t-y_t)^2$ — a fixed shape, quadratic. Generalise it to a power law,

$$ E \;=\; \sum_t \frac{\phi}{p}\,|x_t - y_t|^p \;+\; \sum_t \tfrac12\,q\,(x_{t+1}-x_t)^2, \qquad F_{\text{meas}}(t) \;=\; \phi\,\mathrm{sign}(y_t-x_t)\,|y_t-x_t|^{p-1}. $$

At $p=2$ the force is $\phi(y_t-x_t)$: an ordinary Hookean spring, pull growing linearly with the residual — the Kalman smoother, exactly as before. At $p=1$ the force is $\phi\,\mathrm{sign}(y_t-x_t)$: constant magnitude, regardless of how far off $y_t$ is — the same constant-tension device as the Lasso demo's pulley, and it makes each bead settle at the (process-weighted) median of its neighbours and its own observation. Between $1$ and $2$, the influence function $|r|^{p-1}$ grows sub-linearly in the residual $r$ — a continuous dial between the two.

Point 4 below is a planted outlier. Drag $p$ from $2$ down to $1$ and watch the green (live) curve peel away from the grey dashed line — the ordinary Kalman fit, which the outlier drags noticeably — while the green curve near that point barely moves once $p$ nears $1$: its influence has saturated. Drag any point yourself to plant a bigger or smaller outlier and see the gap widen or close.

$p$ reads as max deviation from Kalman fit

This is exactly the “sub-linear gain” framing of robust M-estimation: the influence function $\psi(r)=|r|^{p-1}\mathrm{sign}(r)$ is linear (unbounded influence) at $p=2$ and constant (bounded influence) at $p=1$. The Huber loss demo takes the other common route to the same place — keep the spring exactly quadratic near zero, but cap its force beyond a threshold — rather than reshaping the whole curve.
