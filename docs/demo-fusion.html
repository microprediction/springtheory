Evidence fusion

The posterior mean is where the pulling stops.

The dark body is the prior. The green bodies are observations. Each carries a mass equal to its precision and is tied by a spring to the white test particle. The particle relaxes to the centre of mass, which is the posterior mean. The bowl above is the potential energy, the negative log posterior. The particle is a ball rolling to its bottom. Drag any body, or add an observation, and watch the equilibrium re-form. The $\rho$ dial threads every observation through a noisy channel of that precision. This replaces its mass $\phi$ by the reduced mass $\phi\rho/(\phi+\rho)$. At the far right the channel is rigid at $\rho=\infty$, and the update is the plain Kalman step.

Everything on screen is computed twice. The readout uses the closed-form update $m' = \big(P^{-1}m + \sum_i \tilde\phi_i y_i\big)\big/\big(P^{-1} + \sum_i \tilde\phi_i\big)$. The particle just integrates $F = ma$ with damping. The two agree. Weighting each observation by its precision is an old habit. Gauss combined observations this way through least squares in the early 1800s, and the inverse-variance weighted average is the one with the least variance. Derivation in the introduction.
